My Journey in Woodworking

  • 2014-2020

    Taking Tech Classes

    My introduction to woodworking began in 7th-grade technology class, laying the groundwork for a technical skill set. The journey progressed through wood construction classes in 10th and 12th grades, where I honed fundamental skills and safety techniques. From the initial stages of learning basic cuts to mastering advanced woodworking techniques, each step marked a technical evolution. As I reflect on this segment of my journey, it represents a progression from foundational knowledge to technical proficiency.

  • 2017-Present

    YouTube Research and Skill Development

    During this next phase, my woodworking journey shifted into high gear as I dedicated countless hours to absorbing insights from woodworking tutorials on YouTube. These online resources became my mentors, teaching me the intricacies of woodworking, safe tool usage, and essential skills that continue to be invaluable. With this knowledge, I established my personal workshop. Each project undertaken in my workspace helped to refine my techniques, implement safety protocols, and further develop my woodworking skills. The combination of virtual learning and personal practice not only deepened my understanding of the craft but also marked a significant chapter in my growth as a woodworker.

  • 2018-Present

    Building My Own Woodshop

    At this point I realized that a personal workshop was needed. To address this need, I revamped a neglected corner of my basement to create a functional workspace. I constructed a sturdy workbench and resolved significant lighting issues in order to lay the groundwork for the expansion of my woodworking. This pivotal step marked the beginning of a deliberate journey to grow both my tool repertoire and woodworking skills. Slowly but steadily, my workshop transformed from a modest corner into a dynamic space, enabling me to take on increasingly complex projects and continue to refine my craft.

  • 2020-2021

    Carpenter at Urban Industrial Design

    In 2020, I took a significant stride in my woodworking journey by joining a local small business specializing in custom furniture, wood slab sales, and the production of floating shelves for retail on Amazon. This employment not only afforded me the opportunity to work efficiently within the demands of the job but also facilitated substantial skill development, heightening my safety awareness and hazard recognition in the workplace. Beyond the hands-on manufacturing processes, I gained insights into crucial small-business operations. The experience enriched my understanding of the end-to-end customer order fulfillment process. Specifically, I contributed to the creation of floating shelves‚Äîcutting boards, drilling, sanding, staining, and meticulously packaging them for shipping. This role not only honed my technical woodworking skills but also developed my ability to collaborate in a dynamic and noisy environment, maintaining high productivity levels while working with others.

  • Present

    Planning New Projects

    In the current phase of my woodworking journey, I am immersed in the planning and conceptualization of future projects. Whether during spare moments or school breaks, I typically have a queue of 5 or 6 projects waiting to be completed. This perpetual stream of creativity drives me to continually learn and experiment with new technologies and techniques, seeking to infuse innovation into every endeavor. Simultaneously, my focus extends beyond the workshop as I delve into growing and enhancing my small woodworking business. This involves an exploration of avenues for improvement, be it in refining existing processes or adopting innovative approaches. The commitment to lifelong learning, coupled with a dedication to pushing the boundaries of my craft, propels me forward, ready to embrace the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead in my woodworking journey.

Live Edge Epoxy River Coffee Table

August 2021

This table represents the fusion of craftsmanship and precision engineering, designed and executed to produce a seamless blend of natural aesthetics and precise craftsmanship. Crafted from walnut slabs, the table features a riverine design filled with blue-tinted epoxy, evoking the fluidity of watercourses. 
                                        
The unique construction process involved creating the table top in two separate pieces, carefully pouring the epoxy inside a mold, planing each walnut slab, and reuniting them seamlessly. The legs of the table introduce a contemporary X-pattern, echoing the structural design found in the beams of the room in which it resides. This intentional nod to the room's architecture not only enhances the table's visual appeal but also establishes a harmonious connection to its surroundings.
                                        
The legs are fabricated from maple, providing both stability and a complementary visual contrast to the rich tones of the walnut. This technical approach to construction, coupled with the artistic incorporation of epoxy, transforms the table into a functional work of art.
                                        
As if bridging the artistic themes between projects, the X-pattern on the sides of this table mirrors the design of my television stand. Crafted from leftover pine sourced from the walls of the room, the television stand's resourceful construction is echoed in the epoxy river table. The symbiotic relationship between these two pieces exemplifies not only a commitment to craftsmanship but also a cohesive design language that ties the room together in a harmonious blend of aesthetics and functionality.

Adirondack Chair with Footrest

                                    
March 2020

                                    

                                    
A big step in my woodworking journey was building an Adirondack chair and accompanying leg rest in a high school woodshop class under the guidance of Mr. Joel Scism. These pieces now reside alongside the epoxy river table and TV stand in the same room, forming a cohesive theme.
                                        
Utilizing the facilities of the school's woodshop, I employed premade templates to trace outlines onto pine boards. The bandsaw was used to precisely cut out each component, ensuring a uniform and accurate execution of the chair and leg rest. The belt sander and spindle sander helped to refine the pieces down to the pencil lines. The chair was then assembled with screws and bolts.
                                        
As a finishing touch, each piece received a coating of spar urethane, enhancing durability and providing protection against the elements.
                                        
The completion of this project held a special significance, as it coincided with the last day ever of high school for me before the onset of the COVID pandemic. The technical precision of these pieces reflects not only a dedication to woodworking craftsmanship but also serves as a tangible reminder of the valuable skills nurtured within the walls of the high school woodshop. Special thanks to Mr. Joel Scism for his guidance and support throughout this endeavor.

I embarked on a journey to a galaxy far, far away with the realization of a childhood dream: a meticulously handcrafted, all-metal replica of Luke Skywalker's iconic green lightsaber from Return of the Jedi (1983). The impetus for this project was a lifelong passion for Star Wars, which instilled in me a desire to create a lightsaber that transcends mere toy replicas.
                                        
The opportunity to bring this vision to life arose during a machining class at the Capital Region BOCES facility near the Albany Airport, facilitated by the generous guidance of Mr. Kody Skeals. His willingness to extend access to the metal lathes and milling machines outside of regular class hours, coupled with invaluable mentorship, transformed this project into a remarkable learning experience.
                                        
The odyssey began with a Solidworks design, crafting a blueprint and ensuring screen-accurate dimensions. A solid block of 6061 aircraft aluminum, measuring 2 inches in diameter and 13 inches in length, served as the canvas for the foundation of the lightsaber hilt. The precision achieved in machining was elevated further through the use of CNC milling machines and a CNC lathe for the highly detailed parts of the project, providing an unprecedented level of detail.
                                        
Returning to the familiar confines of my woodshop, the metal hilt was primed and painted to replicate the authentic appearance of Luke Skywalker's green lightsaber. To bring the weapon to life, a polycarbonate tube, diffuser foam, and LED strips were carefully integrated. A portable charger provided the power, culminating in a lightsaber blade that could be threaded into the hilt, complete with a 3D printed tip for authenticity. A camera case with foam protection for storing the hilt, and a 3D printed shell for safe storage of the blade completed the project.
                                        
This was one of the most rewarding endeavors I've undertaken. A heartfelt and special thanks extends to Mr. Kody Skeals for his exceptional guidance and support throughout this endeavor. His generosity in allowing extra access to the BOCES facility and sharing invaluable tips and insights for precision machining made this project not only possible but an extraordinary and unforgettable experience.

Wooden American Flags

                                    
February 2021-Present

                                    

                                    
In early 2021, I began to craft wooden American flags, drawing inspiration from online tutorials and reference photos. My aim was to bring a personal touch to each flag, combining elements from existing designs with a unique flair. Since the inception of this project, my process has evolved and improved, resulting in distinctive and high-quality flags that resonate with those seeking a patriotic touch for their homes.
                                        
The process begins with premium pine strips which I sand to a smooth finish. To infuse a rustic look, every strip is carefully burned with a torch. The union, constructed separately from a wider panel of premium pine, undergoes the same burning process. Staining follows, with red stain for the red stripes, blue stain for the union, and leaving the natural pine for the white stripes.
                                        
Assembling the 13 stripes and the union requires finesse, utilizing glue and a pneumatic nail gun for secure adhesion. The addition of vertical slats on the back ensures alignment and contributes to the overall sturdiness, a refinement made over time to enhance the flag's durability and flatness.
                                        
One unique aspect that sets my flags apart is the personalized touch. Laser engraving the name of the person or family, my signature, and the date on the back of the union establishes a connection between the flag and its owner. The final touch involves the stars, where a distinctive approach sets my flags apart. Using a stencil that precisely matches the union's size, I affix 3D printed stars into each spot, creating a visual and tactile accent. The intricate design of the stars, reminiscent of military insignia, adds a unique dimension that is unparalleled in the wooden flag market.

Mahogany Side Tables

                                    
July 2022

                                    

                                    
Undertaking a commission from my sister-in-law's aunt, I constructed two identical side tables. The project began with a reference photo provided by the client, which I translated into a scaled blueprint using digital tools.
                                        
To bring the design to life, I printed out templates of both the tabletop and intricate leg geometry, piecing together multiple sheets of paper to ensure proportional accuracy. With the blueprints in hand, I traced the designs onto a panel glue-up of rich mahogany boards.
                                        
Employing the bandsaw, I cut the boards just outside the traced lines, preparing them for the shaping that followed. The intricate geometry of the legs posed both a challenge and a source of reward. Navigating the delicate curves required precision at every step, pushing the boundaries of my woodworking skills. The bandsaw proved indispensable in creating the initial forms, while the combination of the belt and spindle sanders allowed me to sneak up to the pencil line with precision and accuracy.
                                        
The glue-up process, particularly the joining of the three legs to the tabletop, demanded a sophisticated clamping approach to achieve a seamless and stable connection. I was able to leave my mark by laser engraving my signature on the bottom of the tables.
                                        
The final touch involved the application of Danish oil, enhancing the natural beauty of the mahogany and providing a durable and lustrous finish. This project proved true the idea of taking a concept on the internet, and transforming it into reality, based only on images.

Laser Engraved Epoxy Sports Wall Plaque

                                    
June 2022

                                    

                                    
The foundation of this project was a 2 by 4 foot particle board back. To frame this piece, I introduced a border of rich cherry wood, setting the stage for the main attraction‚Äîa trio of live edge slabs. These slabs, harvested and patiently dried, became the focal point of the plaque. Two maple slabs represented my beloved Pittsburgh Steelers and Penguins, while a cherry slab took center stage for the Boston Red Sox.
                                        
I utilized the precision of a laser engraver to emboss the essence of my three favorite teams. Each slab became a visual timeline, adorned with the team name, logo, and a display of championship years crowned by the league's ultimate trophy. A personalized touch manifested as I engraved the name and number of my favorite player beneath each team.
                                        
The three slabs found their place side by side within the cherry wood border. The next step was a Caribbean water-tinted epoxy, encapsulating and embracing the slabs, creating a pleasing visual centerpiece. Boiled linseed oil became the finishing touch, adding a timeless luster to the entire piece. Lastly, I mounted this on my wall above my desk, allowing it to be viewed and observed by all.

Office Desk, Drawer, and Shelving Unit

                                    
December 2020

                                    

                                    
With this project, I set out to create a custom office desk, drawer, and shelving unit for my cousin. Designing a piece that would span wall to wall and floor to ceiling required careful consideration and planning.
                                        
Beginning with precise measurements of the room, I sketched a design that incorporated functional elements and aesthetic appeal. The challenge of determining the number and height of shelves on each side of the window, along with the design intricacies of the drawers flanking the desk, became a rewarding puzzle to solve.
                                        
Translating the design into reality involved creating an organized parts list, detailing the dimensions of each unique piece required for construction. Calculating the necessary plywood sheets, I used a combination of a circular saw edge guide and a table saw to accurately cut the components to size. Sanding each board to a smooth finish, I then applied iron-on maple edge banding to conceal the plywood layers, providing a polished, natural wood appearance.
                                        
Assembling the pieces required precision to ensure 90-degree corners and level shelves. Once the components were united, the entire unit received a carefully chosen paint finish. Adding another layer of complexity, the installation of drawer slides became a crucial step. Ensuring parallel alignment from the design phase through assembly, I incorporated slides for all four drawers and the cabinet carcass. This step guaranteed smooth opening and closing functionality for the drawers. This project still stands the test of time to this day, being used for many purposes.

Preschool Cubby Units

                                    
August 2022

                                    

                                    
Tasked by the principal of St. Mary's Catholic School of Waterford, I undertook the creation of 11 cubby units for their preschool students. Collaborating on size, specifications, and material considerations, we aimed to optimize space and materials, ensuring a practical and durable solution for the young students.
                                        
The project kicked off with careful planning, determining the location and overall dimensions of each cubby unit. Creating a detailed parts list, I specified the unique components, their dimensions, and quantities, paving the way to calculate the necessary plywood sheets. After procuring the materials, using a track saw to break down the plywood into manageable sizes before fine-tuning each piece on the table saw.
                                        
Transporting an entire minivan full of plywood to the school, my dad and I transformed the exterior of the school into a bustling workspace, staining each board with precision. The assembly process became a meticulous yet efficient routine, employing an assembly line approach to ensure consistency across all 11 cubby units. Wood screws brought each cubby to life, identical in size and structure. Once assembled, we maneuvered the cubbies around the school halls, connecting them to strengthen the overall structure.
                                        
The final touch involved adding hooks to each cubby, providing a practical solution for the students to hang their belongings. The assembly line nature of this project, building a substantial number of cubbies, was a testament to efficiency and precision.

All Metal Saruman's Staff from LOTR

                                    
July 2023

                                    

                                    
Immersed in the mesmerizing world of the extended Lord of the Rings trilogy, the character Saruman and his iconic staff left an indelible mark on my creative spirit. Inspired by Christopher Lee's portrayal, and spurred on by the urging of my sister, I decided to embark on the ambitious journey of bringing Saruman's staff to life.
                                        
The research phase began with a deep dive into dimensions, reference photos, and YouTube tutorials showcasing various builds. While many opted for foam or plastic, I set my sights on authenticity, choosing to fashion the entire staff from aluminum.
                                        
Armed with online images, I used Solidworks to carefully trace the forked shape, transforming the collected data into a screen-accurate model. This digital blueprint became a guide as I visualized each part of the staff, ensuring accuracy in every detail. With the design and assembly finalized, I exported the fork templates as .dxf files and used a laser engraver to cut them out in acrylic.
                                        
Turning to eighth-inch aluminum sheet metal, I traced the acrylic templates and cut out the pieces using a metal bandsaw. A metal flush trim bit in my router table brought the intricate details to life, with occasional challenges met and conquered. Sanding and filing followed, refining the metal pieces to eliminate sharp edges and corners.
                                        
The only non-metal component was a 3D printed divider, placed within an aluminum tube to evenly space the eight forks. Each fork found its place under the guidance of the divider, with glue ensuring accuracy and alignment. Primed and painted in a gloss black hue reminiscent of the cinematic masterpiece, the staff began to take its final form.
                                        
The crowning glory came in the form of an LED sphere, a radiant orb nestled amidst the forks. This three-inch diameter sphere, changeable in color via a remote control, brought a dynamic luminosity to the staff. This project is proof of concept that an image from the internet can be used to create real-life objects, and also proved my ability to go from design in Solidworks, to metal - a first for me.

Custom Designed & 3D Printed BMW Logo

                                    
September 2023

                                    

                                    
Captivated by the iconic BMW logo, I embarked on a project to bring this symbol of automotive excellence to life. The vision was to design and construct a giant version of the BMW logo. 
                                        
To create this oversized emblem, I delved into the realm of 3D modeling. Disappointed with the available free models online, I took it upon myself to craft an intricate and faithful representation of the BMW logo. Starting with an image found on the internet, I imported it into a Solidworks sketch, carefully tracing every curve and detail to ensure accuracy.
                                        
The logo, with its distinct silver outline, blue and white center panels, and black insert part, became individual components in my Solidworks assembly. This digital representation allowed me to visualize the entire model before the 3D printing process, ensuring a seamless fit when the parts came together.
                                        
The challenge arose in size‚Äîthe logo was intended to be over 14 inches in diameter, surpassing the capacity of my 3D printer build volume. Employing Autodesk Meshmixer, I sliced the files into manageable sections that could be seamlessly glued back together after printing.
                                        
With filaments of PLA in the exact colors needed, the 3D printer brought each part to life. This eliminated the need for post-print painting, ensuring an authentic representation. Post-printing, a bit of sanding ensured a perfect fit for all components, which were then glued together.

3D Printed Topographic USA Map

                                    
May 2022

                                    

                                    
This project began when I stumbled upon a gem on Thingiverse.com‚Äîa topographic 3D model of the USA map. Intrigued by the prospect of crafting a unique representation of the mainland US, I embarked on the 3D printing.
                                        
Utilizing blue, white, green, and yellow filaments, I brought each state to life layer by layer. The beauty of the design lay in the strategic use of colors, ensuring that no two adjacent states shared the same hue. After a careful sanding and filing process, the states seamlessly interlocked.
                                        
For the canvas, an eighth-inch particle board served as the ideal backer. After a dry fit, I traced the state outlines on the board and took the project outside the conventional mapping boundaries. Armed with painter's tape, I masked off the land sections, leaving the ocean exposed for a coat of blue spray paint.
                                        
With the ocean complete, the 3D-printed states found their permanent home on the backer board, glued in place to recreate the topography of the mainland USA. A final touch was added with a printed compass.
                                        
Special thanks are due to Thingiverse user DerSchweizermaker for crafting these intricate 3D models that allowed me to turn a digital concept into a tangible, wall-mounted topographic tapestry.

Classic Barstool

                                    
July 2019

                                    

                                    
With this project I ventured into the art of constructing a classic barstool. Construction of this piece posed its challenges, especially in crafting the four 1.5-inch by 1.5-inch legs, each set at a compound angle of 7 degrees in both directions.
                                        
Navigating the compound angles required precision on the table saw, tilting the blade while maintaining a specific angle with a miter gauge. Drilling holes for the pegs proved equally intricate, demanding a 7-degree tilt on the drill press table. With careful measurements, I drilled one hole 10 inches from the top and bottom and another 9 inches away, ensuring accuracy for the pegs.
                                        
Dry fitting the pegs into each leg on a flat table served as a visual check, confirming the accuracy of my cutting and drilling operations. Once satisfied with the dry fit, I proceeded to assemble the legs, using a makeshift mold on my workbench to ensure consistent spacing and angles. The 13-inch bottom span of the legs was carefully achieved to maintain uniformity.
                                        
Chamfering the corners added a finishing touch, with the outer corners receiving a larger chamfer for aesthetic appeal. The pegs were then glued in, and the legs were assembled to eliminate any wobble. Crafting the 1.5-inch thick stool top involved using a circle jig on the table saw, transforming a square piece into a rounded seat. A rounded edge was added for comfort on the router table.
                                        
Securing the legs to the seat, sanding ensued before staining the entire barstool. To prevent scratching on wood floors, I dipped the stool feet in Plasti-dip, creating a rubber coating. A final touch involved adding a comfortable pad to enhance the stool's comfort.
                                        
A special thanks to Steve Ramsey of Woodworking for Mere Mortals for his detailed guidance in this project. You can find the tutorial here, a testament to his commitment to sharing woodworking expertise with enthusiasts like myself.

Folding Campfire Seat

                                    
July 2019

                                    

                                    
Inspired by the teachings of Steve Ramsey on his YouTube channel "Woodworking for Mere Mortals," I embarked on my woodworking journey. Among his many projects that captured my attention, one was the Folding Campfire Seat.
                                        
Using freely provided plans and measurements, I printed out the necessary blueprints, ready to transform pine and cedar wood into a compact camping seat The project, outlined in detail in Steve Ramsey's video here, proved both accessible and exciting, perfectly aligned with the tools at my disposal.
                                        
Executing precise cuts with the table and miter saw, I shaped the boards to fit the project's specifications. The clever folding mechanism, a hallmark of this design, demanded accuracy in creating dowel holes. Leveraging my drill press, I ensured the dowels were set in the correct place, facilitating the seat's collapsible nature.
                                        
The finishing touches involved sanding and applying a protective layer of boiled linseed oil, not only safeguarding the wood but also accentuating the natural beauty of the grain.
                                        
The result was a folding campfire seat, ready for adventures and camping excursions. Its portability and flat-folding design made it an ideal companion around the campfire. As I reflect on this project, I extend a special thanks to Steve Ramsey for his years of dedication to sharing woodworking wisdom.
