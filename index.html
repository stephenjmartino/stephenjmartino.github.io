My Journey in Woodworking

  • 2014-2020

    Taking Tech Classes

    It started with 7th grade technology class, and continued with wood construction classes I took in 10th and 12th grade. These classes inspired me to pursue woodworking, taught me the basic skills and safety techniques, and helped me become passionate about woodworking.

  • 2017-Present

    YouTube Research and Skill Development

    I have spent thousands of hours on YouTube watching a multitude of channels and videos to learn how to get into woodworking, how to use tools safely and effectively, and many more skills that have continued to pay off to this day. As I learned, I employed those skills in my own shop to practice and improve my craft.

  • 2018-Present

    Building my own woodshop

    I took a dark and unused part of my basement and got the light fixed to make the space a functional work area. I then used a few basic tools to construct a sturdy workbench. From there, I slowly but surely increased my tool repertoire, my woodworking skills, and my workshop.

  • July 2020-February 2021

    Manufacturer at Urban Industrial Design

    In 2020, I began working a job at a local small business that creates custom furniture, sells wood slabs, and builds floating shelves to be sold on Amazon. Working there enabled me to work in a time efficient manner given the demand of the job. This job helped me develop many skills, including: The business operations component that followed the manufacturing processes, heightening my safety awareness and recognition of potential hazards in the workplace, and comprehension of neccesary steps to fulfill a customer's order.

  • Present

    Planning New Projects

    I am currently planning for and thinking about future project ideas and inspiration while I attend college. Whenever I have spare time or a school break, I usually have 5 or 6 projects waiting for me to complete. I am also looking into growing my small woodworking business.
