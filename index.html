My Journey in Woodworking

  • 2014-2020

    Taking Tech Classes

    My introduction to woodworking began in 7th-grade technology class, laying the groundwork for a technical skill set. The journey progressed through wood construction classes in 10th and 12th grades, where I honed fundamental skills and safety techniques. From the initial stages of learning basic cuts to mastering advanced woodworking techniques, each step marked a technical evolution. Woodworking transitioned beyond a skill acquisition process to a systematic language for precise craftsmanship. Now, as I reflect on this segment of my journey, it encapsulates a progression from foundational knowledge to technical proficiency—a testament to dedication and disciplined skill development in the field of woodworking.

  • 2017-Present

    YouTube Research and Skill Development

    My woodworking journey took a transformative turn as I dedicated countless hours to absorbing insights from woodworking tutorials on YouTube. These online resources became my mentors, teaching me the intricacies of woodworking, safe tool usage, and essential skills that continue to be invaluable. Armed with this knowledge, I established my personal workshop—a space where theory seamlessly translated into hands-on practice. Each project undertaken in my workspace served as a canvas for refining techniques, implementing safety protocols, and pushing the boundaries of my woodworking capabilities. The amalgamation of virtual learning and personal practice not only deepened my understanding of the craft but also marked a significant chapter in my growth as a woodworker.

  • 2018-Present

    Building my own woodshop

    Transitioning into a more hands-on phase of my woodworking journey, I revamped a neglected corner of my basement, solving lighting issues to create a functional workspace. With basic tools in hand, I constructed a sturdy workbench, laying the groundwork for the expansion of my woodworking haven. This pivotal step marked the beginning of a deliberate journey to grow both my tool repertoire and woodworking skills. Slowly but steadily, my workshop transformed from a modest corner into a dynamic space, reflecting not just the physical expansion but also the increasing complexity of my projects and the continual refinement of my craft.

  • 2020-2021

    Carpenter at Urban Industrial Design

    In 2020, I took a significant stride in my woodworking journey by joining a local small business specializing in custom furniture, wood slab sales, and the production of floating shelves for retail on Amazon. This employment not only afforded me the opportunity to work efficiently within the demands of the job but also facilitated substantial skill development, heightening my safety awareness and hazard recognition in the workplace. Beyond the hands-on manufacturing processes, I gained insights into crucial small-business operations. The experience enriched my understanding of the end-to-end customer order fulfillment process. Specifically, I contributed to the creation of floating shelves—cutting the boards, drilling, sanding, staining, and meticulously packaging them for shipping. This role not only honed my technical woodworking skills but also showcased my ability to collaborate in a dynamic and noisy environment, maintaining high productivity levels in tandem with others.

  • Present

    Planning New Projects

    In the current phase of my woodworking journey, I find myself immersed in the planning and conceptualization of future projects, consistently fueled by a reservoir of ideas and inspiration. Whether during spare moments or school breaks, I typically have a queue of 5 or 6 projects waiting to be completed. This perpetual stream of creativity drives me to continually learn and experiment with new technologies and techniques, seeking to infuse innovation into every endeavor. Simultaneously, my focus extends beyond the workshop as I delve into growing and enhancing my small woodworking business. This involves a strategic exploration of avenues for improvement, be it in refining existing processes or adopting innovative approaches. The commitment to lifelong learning, coupled with a dedication to pushing the boundaries of my craft, propels me forward, ready to embrace the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead in my woodworking journey.

Live Edge Epoxy River Coffee Table

August 2021

Explore the fusion of craftsmanship and precision engineering in my epoxy river table, meticulously designed and executed for a seamless blend of natural aesthetics and precise craftsmanship. Crafted from walnut slabs, the table features an entrancing riverine design filled with mesmerizing blue-tinted epoxy, evoking the fluidity of watercourses. This table stands as a testament to dedicated construction.
                                        
The unique construction process involved creating the table top in two separate pieces, meticulously planing each walnut slab, and reuniting them seamlessly. The legs of the table introduce a contemporary X-pattern, echoing the structural design found in the beams of the room. This intentional nod to the room's architecture not only enhances the table's visual appeal but also establishes a harmonious connection to its surroundings.
                                        
The legs, a crucial structural element, are fabricated from robust maple, providing both stability and a complementary visual contrast to the rich tones of the walnut. This technical approach to construction, coupled with the artistic incorporation of epoxy, transforms the table into a functional work of art.
                                        
As if bridging the artistic themes between projects, the X-pattern on the sides of this table mirrors the design of my television stand. Crafted from leftover pine sourced from the walls of the room, the television stand's resourceful construction is echoed in the epoxy river table. The symbiotic relationship between these two pieces exemplifies not only a commitment to craftsmanship but also a cohesive design language that ties the room together in a harmonious blend of aesthetics and functionality.

Adirondack Chair with Footrest

                                    
March 2020

                                    
                                    
Enter the realm of precision woodworking with my Adirondack chair and accompanying leg rest, meticulously crafted in a high school woodshop class under the guidance of Mr. Joel Scism. These pieces now reside alongside the epoxy river table and TV stand in the same room, forming a cohesive theme of handcrafted excellence.
                                        
Utilizing the facilities of the school's woodshop, I employed premade templates to trace outlines onto pine boards. The bandsaw became the tool of choice to precisely cut out each component, ensuring a uniform and accurate execution of the chair and leg rest. A precision sanding process, facilitated by the belt sander and spindle sander, refined each piece to meet the exacting standards set by the traced lines.
                                        
The assembly of the Adirondack chair and leg rest involved a systematic use of wood screws, creating a robust and stable structure that seamlessly blends form and function. As a finishing touch, each piece received a coating of spar urethane, enhancing durability and providing protection against the elements.
                                        
The completion of this project held a special significance, as it coincided with the last day ever of high school for me before the onset of the COVID pandemic. The technical precision employed in the creation of these pieces reflects not only a dedication to woodworking craftsmanship but also serves as a tangible reminder of the resilience and skill nurtured within the walls of the high school woodshop. Special thanks to Mr. Joel Scism for his guidance and support throughout this endeavor.

This television stand is not just a functional piece but a celebration of resourcefulness, craftsmanship, and the art of giving purpose to reclaimed materials.

Machined Luke Skywalker's Lightsaber

                                    
June 2023
.

                                    
                                    
Embark on a journey to a galaxy far, far away with the realization of a childhood dream: a meticulously handcrafted, all-metal replica of Luke Skywalker's iconic green lightsaber from Return of the Jedi (1983). The inception of this project found its catalyst in a lifelong passion for Star Wars, with the pursuit of authenticity driving the desire to create a lightsaber that transcends mere toy replicas.
                                        
The opportunity to bring this vision to life arose during a machining class at the Capital Region BOCES facility near the Albany Airport, facilitated by the generous guidance of Mr. Kody Skeals. His willingness to extend access to the metal lathes and milling machines outside of regular class hours, coupled with invaluable mentorship, transformed this project into a remarkable learning experience. 
                                        
The odyssey began with a Solidworks design, crafting a blueprint and ensuring screen-accurate dimensions. A solid block of 6061 aircraft aluminum, measuring 2 inches in diameter and 13 inches in length, served as the canvas for the foundation of the lightsaber hilt. The precision achieved in machining was elevated further through the use of CNC milling machines and a CNC lathe for the highly detailed parts of the project, providing an unprecedented level of detail.
                                        
Returning to the familiar confines of my woodshop, the metal hilt was primed and painted to replicate the authentic appearance of Luke Skywalker's green lightsaber. To bring the weapon to life, a polycarbonate tube, diffuser foam, and LED strips were carefully integrated. A portable charger provided the power, culminating in a lightsaber blade that could be threaded into the hilt, complete with a 3D printed tip for authenticity. A camera case with foam protection for storing the hilt, and a 3D printed shell for safe storage of the blade showcased the attention to detail invested in every aspect.
                                        
This project, one of the most rewarding endeavors I've undertaken, extended beyond the creation of the lightsaber. The culmination of this project, with its intricate metalwork and the vibrant glow of the lightsaber blade, stands as a testament to the fusion of craftsmanship and technology. A heartfelt and special thanks extend to Mr. Kody Skeals for his exceptional guidance and support throughout this endeavor. His generosity in allowing extra access to the BOCES facility and sharing invaluable tips and tricks for precision machining made this project not only possible but an extraordinary and unforgettable experience. Without his expertise, this realization of a childhood dream would not have been possible, making this lightsaber not just a symbolic homage to Luke Skywalker but also a tribute to the collaborative spirit of creation.

Mahoghany Side Tables

                                    
July 2022
.

                                    
                                    
Undertaking a commission from my sister-in-law's aunt, I embarked on the construction of two identical side tables, each a testament to precision woodworking and attention to detail. The project began with a reference photo provided by the client, which I skillfully translated into a scaled blueprint using digital tools.
                                        
To bring the design to life, I printed out templates of both the tabletop and intricate leg geometry, artfully piecing together multiple sheets of paper to ensure proportional accuracy. With the blueprints in hand, I traced the designs onto a panel glue-up of rich mahogany boards, setting the stage for the creation of these elegant side tables. 
                                        
Employing the bandsaw, I cut the boards just outside the traced lines, setting the stage for the shaping that followed. The intricate geometry of the legs posed both a challenge and a source of reward. Navigating the delicate curves required precision at every step, pushing the boundaries of my woodworking skills. The bandsaw proved indispensable in creating the initial forms, while the combination of the belt and spindle sanders allowed me to sneak up to the pencil line for unparalleled precision and accuracy.
                                        
The complexity of the table legs presented both a challenge and a source of reward. The glue-up process, particularly the joining of the three legs to the tabletop, demanded a sophisticated clamping approach to achieve a seamless and stable connection.
                                        
A personal touch was added to each table, as my signature was laser engraved on the underside of the tabletops—a subtle mark of the artisan responsible for these meticulously crafted pieces. The final touch involved the application of Danish oil, enhancing the natural beauty of the mahogany and providing a durable and lustrous finish.
                                        
These mahogany side tables stand as a testament to the fusion of craftsmanship and technology, where digital precision meets hands-on skill. The result is not just functional furniture but a pair of timeless pieces that showcase the artistry and dedication invested in their creation.

Office Desk, Drawer, and Shelving Unit

                                    
December 2020
.

                                    
                                    
Embarking on a special commission from my cousin, I set out to create a custom office desk, drawer, and shelving unit for my dad's childhood home, infusing the project with sentimental value. Designing a piece that would span wall to wall and floor to ceiling in my dad's childhood bedroom required careful consideration and planning.
                                        
Beginning with precise measurements of the room, I sketched a design that incorporated functional elements and aesthetic appeal. The challenge of determining the number and height of shelves on each side of the window, along with the design intricacies of the drawers flanking the desk, became a rewarding puzzle to solve.
                                        
Translating the design into reality involved creating an organized parts list, detailing the dimensions of each unique piece required for construction. Calculating the necessary plywood sheets, I used a combination of a circular saw edge guide and a table saw to accurately cut the components to size. Sanding each board to a smooth finish, I then applied iron-on maple edge banding to conceal the plywood layers, providing a polished, natural wood appearance.
                                        
Assembling the pieces required precision to ensure 90-degree corners and level shelves. Once the components were united, the entire unit received a carefully chosen paint finish. Adding another layer of complexity, the installation of drawer slides became a crucial step. Ensuring parallel alignment from the design phase through assembly, I meticulously incorporated slides for all four drawers and the cabinet carcass. This step, demanding precision and accuracy, guaranteed smooth opening and closing functionality for the drawers.
                                        
This custom office unit not only serves a functional purpose but also stands as a labor of love, connecting the past with the present in the very room where my father once lived.

Preschool Cubby Units

                                    
August 2022
.

                                    
                                    
Tasked by my cousin, then the principal at St. Mary's Catholic School of Waterford, I undertook the creation of 11 cubby units for their preschool students. Collaborating on size, specifications, and material considerations, we aimed to optimize space and materials, ensuring a practical and durable solution for the young students.
                                        
The project kicked off with careful planning, determining the location and overall dimensions of each cubby unit. Creating a detailed parts list, I specified the unique components, their dimensions, and quantities, paving the way to calculate the necessary plywood sheets. After procuring the materials, I set up a makeshift workshop in my driveway, using a track saw to break down the plywood into manageable sizes before fine-tuning each piece on the table saw.
                                        
Transporting an entire minivan full of plywood to the school, my dad and I transformed the exterior into a bustling workspace, staining each board with precision. Passersby witnessed our dedication as we worked outdoors, staining hundreds of boards to achieve a uniform and polished finish.
                                        
The assembly process became a meticulous yet efficient routine, employing an assembly line approach to ensure consistency across all 11 cubby units. Wood screws brought each cubby to life, identical in size and structure. Once assembled, we maneuvered the cubbies around the school halls, strategically connecting them to strengthen the overall structure.
                                        
The final touch involved adding hooks to each cubby, providing a practical solution for the students to hang their belongings. The assembly line nature of this project, building a substantial number of cubbies, was a testament to efficiency and precision. As the last screw was tightened, the satisfaction of knowing that over 40 students would soon have a designated space for their coats, bags, books, and lunches made the entire process incredibly rewarding.

Wooden American Flags

                                    
February 2021-Present
.

                                    
                                    
In early 2021, I embarked on a journey to craft wooden American flags, drawing inspiration from online tutorials and reference photos. My aim was to bring a personal touch to each flag, combining elements from existing designs with my unique flair. Since the inception of this project, my process has evolved and improved, resulting in distinctive and high-quality flags that resonate with those seeking a patriotic touch for their homes.
                                        
The process begins with precision, cutting premium pine strips to length and sanding them for a smooth finish. To infuse a rustic charm, every strip is carefully burned with a torch. The union, constructed separately from wider panels of premium pine, undergoes the same burning process. Staining follows, with red stain for the red stripes, blue stain for the union, and leaving the natural pine for the white stripes.
                                        
Assembling the 13 stripes and the union requires finesse, utilizing glue and a pneumatic nail gun for secure adhesion. The addition of vertical slats on the back ensures alignment and contributes to the overall sturdiness, a refinement made over time to enhance the flag's durability and flatness.
                                        
One unique aspect that sets my flags apart is the personalized touch. Laser engraving the name of the person or family, my signature, and the date on the back of the union establishes a profound connection between the flag and its owner. The final touch involves the stars, where a distinctive approach sets my flags apart. Using a stencil that precisely matches the union's size, I affix 3D printed stars into each spot, creating a visual and tactile accent that elevates the entire flag. The intricate design of the stars, reminiscent of military insignia, adds a unique and unparalleled dimension.
                                        
This dedication to craftsmanship and innovation has carved a niche, with customers appreciating the surprise and delight of the 3D printed stars. Witnessing my flags find a place in others' homes is an honor, and I take pride in providing a service that celebrates patriotism and craftsmanship.

Laser Engraved Epoxy Sports Wall Plaque

                                    
June 2022
.

                                    
                                    
Transforming my bedroom wall, I embarked on the creation of a unique sports wall plaque that replaced my bulletin board above the desk. The foundation of this project was a 2 by 4 foot particle board back, a canvas awaiting a fusion of craftsmanship and sports fervor.
                                        
To frame this homage, I introduced a border of rich cherry wood, setting the stage for the main attraction—a trio of live edge slabs. These slabs, harvested and patiently dried, became the focal point of the plaque. Two maple slabs stood tall for my beloved Pittsburgh Steelers and Penguins, while a cherry slab took center stage for the Boston Red Sox.

The final flourish was a pour of Caribbean water-tinted epoxy, encapsulating and embracing the slabs, creating a stunning visual centerpiece.
                                        
Boiled linseed oil became the finishing touch, adding a timeless luster to the entire piece. As the last stroke of craftsmanship, the plaque found its home on my bedroom wall—a testament to my unwavering passion for the Pittsburgh Steelers, Penguins, and Boston Red Sox, immortalized in a stunning fusion of woodwork and sports fandom.

All Metal Saruman's Staff from LOTR

                                    
July 2023
.

                                    
                                    
Immersed in the mesmerizing world of the extended Lord of the Rings trilogy, the character Saruman and his iconic staff left an indelible mark on my creative spirit. Inspired by Christopher Lee's portrayal, and spurred on by the urging of my sister, I decided to embark on the ambitious journey of bringing Saruman's staff to life.
                                        
Initially dismissing the idea due to a packed schedule, the allure of the project proved too enticing to resist. The research phase unfolded, with a deep dive into dimensions, reference photos, and YouTube tutorials showcasing various builds. While many opted for foam or plastic, I set my sights on authenticity, choosing to fashion the entire staff from aluminum.
                                        
Armed with online images, I employed Solidworks to carefully trace the forked shape, transforming the collected data into a screen-accurate model. This digital blueprint became a guiding light as I visualized each part of the staff, ensuring accuracy in every detail. With the design and assembly finalized, I exported the fork templates as .dxf files and turned to Siena College's laser engraver to cut them out in acrylic—a precise and invaluable step in the process.
                                        
Turning to eighth-inch aluminum sheet metal, I traced the acrylic templates, deftly cut out the pieces on Siena's metal bandsaw, and returned to my woodshop. Here, a metal flush trim bit in my router table brought the intricate details to life, with occasional challenges met and conquered. Sanding and filing followed, refining the metal pieces to eliminate sharp edges and corners.

Custom Designed & 3D Printed BMW Logo

                                    
September 2023
.

                                    
                                    
Captivated by the iconic BMW logo, I embarked on a project to bring this symbol of automotive elegance to life in grand proportions. The vision was to design and construct a giant version of the BMW logo, a testament to my admiration for the brand, regardless of the possibility of owning a BMW car in the future.
                                        
To create this oversized emblem, I delved into the realm of 3D modeling. Disappointed with the available free models online, I took it upon myself to craft an intricate and faithful representation of the BMW logo. Starting with an image found on the internet, I imported it into a Solidworks sketch, carefully tracing every curve and detail to ensure accuracy.
                                        
The logo, with its distinct silver outline, blue and white center panels, and black insert part, became individual components in my Solidworks assembly. This digital representation allowed me to visualize the entire model before the 3D printing process, ensuring a seamless fit when the parts came together.
                                        
The challenge arose in size—the logo was intended to be over 14 inches in diameter, surpassing the capacity of my 3D printer build volume. Employing Autodesk Meshmixer, I sliced the files into manageable sections that could be seamlessly glued back together after printing.
                                        
With filaments of PLA in the exact colors needed, the 3D printer brought each part to life. This eliminated the need for post-print painting, ensuring an authentic representation. Post-printing, a bit of sanding ensured a perfect fit for all components, which were then glued together.
                                        
This project not only tested my design skills and understanding of tolerances but also provided a tangible realization of a recognizable and revered automotive emblem. Looking ahead, considering the ownership of the 3D files, I am exploring the possibility of selling these files online, offering enthusiasts the chance to 3D print their own larger-than-life BMW logo.

3D Printed Topographic USA Map

                                    
May 2022
.

                                    
                                    
In the pursuit of a captivating project, I stumbled upon a gem on Thingiverse.com—a topographic 3D model of the USA map. Intrigued by the prospect of crafting a unique representation of the mainland, I embarked on a 3D printing adventure.
                                        
Utilizing blue, white, green, and yellow filaments, I brought each state to life layer by layer. The beauty of the design lay in the strategic use of colors, ensuring that no two adjacent states shared the same hue. After a careful sanding and filing process, the states seamlessly interlocked, forming a stunning jigsaw puzzle.
                                        
For the canvas, eighth-inch particle board served as the ideal backer. After a dry fit, I traced the state outlines on the board and took the project outside the conventional mapping boundaries. Armed with painter's tape, I masked off the land sections, leaving the ocean exposed for a coat of blue spray paint.
                                        
With the ocean complete, the 3D-printed states found their permanent home on the backer board, glued in place to recreate the topography of the mainland USA. A final touch was added with a printed compass, infusing a geographic essence into the masterpiece.
                                        
Special thanks are due to Thingiverse user DerSchweizermaker for crafting these intricate 3D models that allowed me to turn a digital concept into a tangible, wall-mounted topographic tapestry. Now proudly displayed in my basement, this project stands as both a testament to technological creativity and a nod to the diverse landscapes that define the United States.
